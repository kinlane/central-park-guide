# Central Park Guide for Sports Fans — Fri May 29 – Thu Jun 4

Hey sports fans — this is a heavy league weekend stacked on top of two headline moments: the **CRCA Central Park Racing Series crit on East Drive at dawn Saturday**, and the **CP Sports Summer Program** opening Monday at South Dead Road, which puts youth sports back on the park's permit map for the season. Plus a quirky one worth walking down to: **Model Yacht Racing at Conservatory Water all day Saturday** — the kind of thing that's been happening at Kerbs Boathouse for over a century and still pulls a crowd.

## Weather this week

Cool and breezy Saturday (66°F, N winds 12–15) for great field conditions, a pristine sunny Sunday (77°F), then mild but unsettled Mon–Tue with afternoon thunderstorm chances. Check field-closure updates Monday and Tuesday afternoons — a wet outfield can shift the evening league rotations off North Meadow and Heckscher fast.

## Fri May 29 — Corporate Challenge teardown + a normal Friday league night

The J.P. Morgan Corporate Challenge race ran Wed and Thu nights, so Friday is just staging strike at **South Dead Road B + Bandshell Plaza** — trucks and fencing coming out through midday. Doesn't affect field play, but the south end will look busier than usual into the afternoon. League-wise it's a standard Friday: roughly **24 Baseball, 36 Softball, 36 T-Ball, and 58 Tennis** matchups rotate through Heckscher, North Meadow, Great Lawn, and the East 102nd Clay Oval into the evening.

## Sat May 30, 4:00–8:30 AM — CRCA crit on East Drive

The headline sports event of the week. **Central Park Racing Association** runs its weekly crit on East Drive starting at 4 AM, peloton rolling through the 5–6:30 AM window, loop clean by 9. If you've never watched a CP crit, the climb up Cat Hill is the spot — racers come through tight, hard, and fast right before sunrise. Stand on the east-side path between 72nd and 79th and you'll get the whole field three or four times. After the crit wraps, the park flips into a packed league day: **32 Baseball, 46 Softball, 49 T-Ball, plus Soccer and Kickball** rotations across North Meadow, Great Lawn, and Heckscher. Heads-up that **East Meadow is closed all weekend** — anything that normally lives over there has been moved.

## Sat May 30, 9 AM–3 PM — Model Yacht Racing at Conservatory Water

If you want to see a sport that doesn't look like any of the others on this email, walk down to **Kerbs Boathouse** Saturday morning. The Central Park Model Yacht Club has been racing radio-controlled and free-sail boats on Conservatory Water since the 1870s. Six hours of racing, free to watch, the boats are gorgeous up close. Pair it with a coffee from the east-side carts and you've got a morning.

## Sun May 31 — Pristine day, league play all day

Best weather day of the week (77°F, sunny, light wind) and the league schedule reflects it: **48 Softball, 49 T-Ball, 19 Baseball** games rotate through North Meadow and Heckscher start-to-finish. East Meadow still closed. No race, no crit, no special program — just a clean Sunday of field sports under the best sky we'll see this week. Get there early if you're spectating; the picnic crowds will arrive by noon.

## Mon Jun 1 — CP Sports Summer Program opens at South Dead Road

The season starts. **CP Sports Summer Program** runs South Dead Road A from **8:30 AM–noon** and South Dead Road B from **12:30–4 PM**, weekday recurring through the week. It's a youth-sports program — not a big footprint, but it's the first standing weekday block at the south end since school programs wound down. Layer that on top of standard Monday league rotations (Baseball, Softball, T-Ball mostly) and East Meadow's final maintenance day, and the south end stays active through dinner. **Watch the radar after 2 PM** — afternoon thunderstorm chances could pull evening league games off North Meadow.

## Tue Jun 2 – Thu Jun 4 — Weekday CP Sports + league rotations

CP Sports keeps running at South Dead Road A (AM) and B (PM) each weekday. League play settles into the regular weekday mix — Softball-heavy evenings, T-Ball afternoons, Baseball on the Heckscher diamonds. Tuesday brings another PM rain risk; Wednesday is a slight chance; Thursday warms to 83°F under full sun (first 80s of June, and a heat wave is building for Fri Jun 5). **Wed Jun 3** has Wild West Playground Lawn closed to 2 PM (west side mid-park, minor); **Thu Jun 4** has the East 106th St South Mount closed to 2 PM (far north-east, won't affect main fields).

## Quick recap

- **Sat 5/30, 4–8:30 AM** — CRCA crit on East Drive. The week's headline. Watch from Cat Hill.
- **Sat 5/30, 9 AM–3 PM** — Model Yacht Racing at Conservatory Water. Quirky and worth it.
- **Mon 6/1** — CP Sports Summer Program opens at South Dead Road A + B (recurring all week).
- **Sat + Sun + Mon** — East Meadow closed; east-side league play is displaced.
- **Sat 5/30** — ~130+ league games rotate through North Meadow / Heckscher / Great Lawn. Busy day.
- **Sun 5/31** — Best weather of the week (77°F sunny), heaviest single-day league slate.
- **Mon + Tue PM** — Afternoon storm risk; check field-closure updates before evening games.
- **Thu 6/4** — 83°F, first 80s of June, heat wave building into next weekend.

Play ball,
