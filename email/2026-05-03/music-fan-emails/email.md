# Central Park Music & Performance: Week of May 3–9

Hey music fans,

The Bandshell is busy this week and there's a few off-the-beaten-path performances worth catching. Here's the rundown.

## Today (Sun May 3)

**Three things happening, all free:**

- **Arise New York** — Bandshell Plaza, 12–6 PM. Outdoor festival lineup, full afternoon.
- **Copper Hills HS Dance Flash Mob** — Bethesda Fountain Terrace, 12:15–1 PM. Short, joyful, photographable.
- **Central Park International Folkdancers** — King Jagiallo Plaza (75th & CPE), 11 AM–7 PM. Live music, costumed dancing, traditional repertoire from around the world.

Easy plan: catch the flash mob at 12:15 at Bethesda, walk up to the Bandshell for Arise, then drift over to King Jagiallo in the late afternoon if you've got energy.

## Mon May 4 — Escanaba HS Band

The Escanaba HS Band performs at the Bandshell, 2–3 PM. Touring high school band — short set, but a sweet weekday lunchtime stop if you're nearby.

## Tue May 5 — World Prayer for Peace, Unity and Diversity

Bandshell Plaza, 11 AM–7 PM. Daylong program of music, speakers, and performances from a wide range of communities. Worth dropping in for an hour.

## Fri May 8 — The MGP Experience and Fandango NYC

Bandshell Plaza, 1–4 PM. The kind of Bandshell afternoon that justifies skipping out on work early.

## A few quieter notes

- **Yoga in Central Park: the Artist Series** at Mineral Springs (Tue 5/5, 6:30–7:30 PM). It's a yoga class, but the "Artist Series" framing means there's a live musician — could be a nice low-key evening.
- The Bandshell's quiet on Wed and Thu — good days to walk through without crowds.
