# Central Park Wellness Week: May 3–9

Hi there,

Two scheduled wellness events this week, plus some thoughts on where to find quiet on busy days. Here's the week.

## The two scheduled classes

**Tue May 5 — Yoga in Central Park: the Artist Series**
Mineral Springs (just behind Sheep Meadow), 6:30–7:30 PM. The Artist Series pairs the yoga class with a live musician — gentle ambient performance during practice. Bring your own mat. Donation-based.

**Sat May 9 — Bondd Pilates Mother's Day Pop-up**
Great Lawn South West Lawn, 11 AM–1 PM. Outdoor pilates with a Mother's Day theme. Bring a mat or towel; the lawn is firm but you'll want some padding.

## Where to find calm this week

The park has several big events, but plenty of space stays peaceful. If you want a quiet morning practice, walk, or sit:

- **Conservatory Garden** (105th & 5th) — formal, walled, completely calm all week. Tulips at peak.
- **Hallett Nature Sanctuary** — small, quiet, magical. Open during limited hours, check signage.
- **The Ramble** — peak bird migration is now; quiet movement here is its own meditation.
- **North Woods & Ravine** — feels like upstate New York. Almost no events scheduled here all week.

## What to avoid for a calm experience

- **South end of the park, Sun 5/3 morning**: TD Five Boro Bike Tour finish, 7 AM–noon. Crowds, vehicles, noise.
- **Bandshell area, multiple days**: Sun, Mon, Tue, Fri all have programmed events. Stay 200 yards away if you want quiet.
- **West Drive at 72nd, Sat 5/9 afternoon**: Skate Circle returns 2–7 PM. Fun if you want energy, draining if you don't.
- **5th Ave at 97th, weekday mornings**: daily Olmsted Luncheon setup midnight–noon. Crowded sidewalk.

## Mother's Day weekend

Sun May 10 (just after this week) is Mother's Day. The park gets busy late morning but is **unusually peaceful before 9 AM** — one of the best mornings of the year for an early walk or sit.
