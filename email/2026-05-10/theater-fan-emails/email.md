# Julius Caesar enters Week 2 at The Pool: May 10–16

Hey theater fans,

Julius Caesar is in its second week at The Pool, and this is the one to plan a night around. Here's what's on.

## Still running — Julius Caesar at The Pool

**Daily through Sun May 19, 10 AM–9 PM** at The Pool Lawns (100th–103rd, west side, just inside the park from CPW). Bring a blanket. The long daily window covers production setup, tech, rehearsal, and performance — check the company's listing for actual show times.

A few late-arriver notes if you haven't been yet:

- **Sightlines** are best from the north and east lawns, looking south across the water
- **Get there 30+ min early** on weekend evenings; the lawn fills up
- **The light is best around 7:30–8 PM** — golden-hour staging when the show is in full swing
- It cools off quickly at The Pool once the sun drops below the western tree line — layers

## Sun May 10 — Folkdance theater + Mother's Day

**Central Park International Folkdancers** at King Jagiallo Plaza, 11 AM–7 PM. Costumed performance with live music — closer to dance theater than concert. If you like physical, traditional performance, drop in.

Mother's Day means **Bow Bridge, Bethesda, Belvedere Castle Terrace, Cop Cot** all have stacked private ceremonies. Less theater, more pageantry — but worth a wander if you're nearby.

## Wed May 13 — Bill Popp at Bandshell

Not theater proper, but **Bill Popp and the Tapes at Bandshell, 5–7 PM** — veteran NYC indie rock outfit, evening Bandshell vibes. Pair with a 7:30 PM walk to The Pool for Julius Caesar if you want a full Wednesday-night plan.

## Looking ahead

- **Barefoot Shakespeare** opens at the Bandshell May 28–30 — we'll cover closer to the date
- Watch The Pool after May 19 to see if another production claims the space
