# Central Park Guide for Sports Fans — Mon Jun 1 – Sun Jun 7

Hey sports fans — heavy league weekend stacked on top of the first 90°F stretch of 2026, which means field conditions and heat protocols are the whole story Sat afternoon. Model Yacht Racing fills Conservatory Water 9–3, Bowling Greens is fully closed for maintenance, and CRCA's Club Series may run its usual Saturday dawn crit on East Drive (Jun 6 unconfirmed on their public calendar — check crca.net). East Meadow goes offline Sat–Sun (plus a Mon maintenance hit), so east-side field play shifts west.

## Weather this week

Cool and clean Mon–Wed in the low-to-mid 70s — ideal play conditions across every field — then a sharp climb to **90°F Fri and Sat** before rain rolls in Sun (84°F). Sat afternoon games are the ones to watch: expect heat-protocol calls, modified inning lengths, and outright cancellations once the sun comes over the third-base line.

## Mon Jun 1 — CP Sports Summer Program opens at South Dead Road

Weekday youth-sports program kicks off its summer run: **Session A 8:30 AM–noon, Session B 12:30–4 PM at South Dead Road**, every weekday through Fri. Adult and youth league play is in normal-busy weekday shape — roughly 20 Baseball, 40+ Softball, and 40+ T-Ball games scattered across the park. **East Meadow closed all day for maintenance** — Heckscher and North Meadow absorb the east-side overflow.

## Tue Jun 2 — Weekday league rhythm, ideal conditions

Same weekday roster: ~20 Baseball, 40+ Softball, 40+ T-Ball. CP Sports Summer Program continues at South Dead Road A + B. 75°F and sunny — best play conditions of the week. East Meadow back open.

## Wed Jun 3 — Last cool day before the heat builds

Weekday league play continues at full volume. CP Sports Summer Program at South Dead Road. **Wild West Playground Lawn closed to 2 PM** for maintenance — minor footprint, not a field hit. 80°F and the warming trend starts, but still comfortable through the evening session.

## Thu Jun 4 — Warming trend, watch hydration

Full weekday rosters across Baseball, Softball, T-Ball. CP Sports Summer Program at South Dead Road. **East 106 St South Mount closed to 2 PM** for maintenance. 84°F and sunny — first day to seriously top off water bottles between innings.

## Fri Jun 5 — Heat wave begins (90°F), CP Sports Summer Program wraps the week

Weekday league rosters at full volume but **first 90°F day of 2026** — afternoon Session B (12:30–4) at South Dead Road will be running heat checks. Adult evening softball after 6 PM is the only comfortable window. Hydrate aggressively, shade between innings, expect umpires to call extra water breaks.

## Sat Jun 6 — Heavy league day meets the heat dome

The week's biggest day on the fields runs straight into the heat-wave peak: **31 Baseball, 36 Softball, 36 T-Ball, and 4 Kickball games** scheduled across the park. Pre-noon games are fine; **afternoon games at 90°F under full sun are the heat-protocol/cancellation candidates** — check your league's hotline before you leave the house. Cycling note: CRCA's Club Series may run its usual Saturday dawn crit on East Drive (Jun 6 not confirmed on their public calendar — check crca.net). If on, loop clean by 9. Quirky pick of the week: **Model Yacht Racing at Kerbs Boathouse / Conservatory Water 9 AM–3 PM** — classic CP tradition, shaded benches, worth the walk over. Closures hit hard: **Bowling Greens closed all day for maintenance**, and **East Meadow closed** — east-side games rerouted.

## Sun Jun 7 — Busiest league day of the week, rain risk late

The single biggest league-play day of the week: **17 Baseball, 53 Softball, 53 T-Ball, and 4 Kickball games**. 84°F with chance of afternoon showers — cooler than Sat but watch the radar for late-afternoon delays. **East Meadow still closed** through the weekend, so North Meadow and Heckscher carry the load. No loop events Sunday — clean cycling/running day around the league footprint.

- **Sat dawn:** Check crca.net for a possible Club Series crit on East Drive; if on, loop clean by 9.

Play through the heat (or don't),
