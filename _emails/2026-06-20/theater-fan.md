# Central Park Theater — Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26

*Quick note: this edition usually lands Friday night — I let it slip a day again, so here it is Saturday morning, covering Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26.*

Hey theater fans — this is a **front-loaded Shakespeare weekend**: two free outdoor *Julius Caesar* productions running at once, *The Taming of the Shrew* at Summit Rock on Saturday, and then the **Public Theater Mobile Unit at the Great Lawn on Tuesday**. The back half of the week goes dark outdoors — so make your plans for Sat, Sun, and Tue.

## Weather this week

A sunny, dry weekend (Sat 84°, Sun 83° with a small late-day storm risk) is ideal for outdoor seating, then Mon–Tue turn wet (showers and thunderstorms, 77°/80°), Wednesday clears (84°), and Thu–Fri stay warm and unsettled (80°/83°). Tuesday's Mobile Unit show is the one with rain exposure — check the Public Theater's status before heading to the Great Lawn, and bring a poncho just in case.

## Sat 6/20 — A Shakespeare triple-header

The week's biggest theater day. **The Taming of the Shrew plays Summit Rock at 2 PM**; **Julius Caesar (Part 2 of 2) runs at The Pool from 5 PM**; and **NY Classical's *The Tragedy of Julius Caesar* opens at the West 103rd Street entrance at 7 PM** — two different free *Caesar* stagings the same weekend, plus a comedy in the afternoon. For something adjacent, **Tango at the Shakespeare Statue (6 PM)** turns the Mall into a performance of its own. 84° and clear — perfect for a blanket and a long afternoon-into-evening.

## Sun 6/21 — Both Caesars return

83°, sunny early. **Julius Caesar (Part 2 of 2) plays The Pool at 5 PM** and **NY Classical's Julius Caesar runs at the W 103rd St entrance at 7 PM** again — your second chance at either if Saturday filled up. Get seated before the small afternoon storm chance and you're set.

## Mon 6/22 — Dark (and wet)

Showers and thunderstorms, 77°. No outdoor theater on the calendar — a good night to read the script before Tuesday or catch something indoors.

## Tue 6/23 — Public Theater Mobile Unit at the Great Lawn, 6 PM

The week's marquee. **The Public Theater's Mobile Unit performs free at the Great Lawn at 6 PM** — the company's stripped-down, community-touring Shakespeare. Rain showers are likely earlier (80°), so confirm the show is on before you go. (Bonus for the music-curious: The Knights play the Naumburg Bandshell the same evening at 7:30.)

## Wed 6/24 — Dark, but the prettiest night to be out

84° and sunny — no theater on the books, ironically the best-weather evening of the week. If the Mobile Unit or a Caesar tempts you to a repeat, this is the night you'd wish had a show.

## Thu 6/25 — Dark

80° with a chance of storms and nothing scheduled outdoors. A quiet night for the outdoor stages.

## Fri 6/26 — Dark

83° with scattered showers and no outdoor productions listed. The week's theater lives entirely in its first half.

## Quick recap

- **Sat 6/20 is the big day:** Taming of the Shrew at Summit Rock (2 PM), Julius Caesar at The Pool (5 PM), NY Classical's Julius Caesar at W 103rd (7 PM), Tango at the Shakespeare Statue (6 PM).
- **Sun 6/21:** both Julius Caesar productions return (5 PM at The Pool, 7 PM at W 103rd).
- **Tue 6/23, 6 PM:** Public Theater Mobile Unit at the Great Lawn — the marquee; check for rain.
- **Mon, Wed–Fri are dark outdoors** — and the Delacorte/Shakespeare in the Park season still isn't confirmed in our calendar.
