# Central Park Wellness — Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26

*Quick note: this edition usually lands Friday night — I let it slip a day again, so here it is Saturday morning, covering Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26.*

Hi wellness seekers — this week holds the **summer solstice (Sun 6/21)**, the longest light of the year: sunrise near 5:25 AM, sunset past 8:30 PM. That's your invitation to claim the early and late edges of the day, when the park is quiet and the light is soft. Programming is sparse — one prayer service Monday — so the practice this week is solitude and timing.

## Weather this week

A sunny, dry weekend (Sat 84°, Sun 83°), a wet Mon–Tue (showers and thunderstorms, 77°/80°), a clear 84° Wednesday, and warm, unsettled Thu–Fri (80°/83°). For a contemplative practice the rain is a gift: Mon–Tue mornings in the Conservatory Garden or under the Ramble canopy will be almost empty — go early, go slow, and let the quiet do the work.

## Sat 6/20 — Dawn light before the crowds

84° and sunny. With the year's earliest sunrises, a 5:30–7 AM walk gives you Bow Bridge, the Mall, and the Reservoir nearly to yourself before a warm Saturday fills the park. The free-music and market activity builds at Rumsey and the southwest gates by midday — a location heads-up to steer *away* from if you want stillness; the north end and Conservatory Garden stay calm.

## Sun 6/21 — Solstice: bookend the longest day

83°, sunny early with a chance of an afternoon storm. Make the most of the solstice at both ends — a dawn sit at Summit Rock or the Reservoir, and a long-light evening (sunset ~8:31 PM) at Belvedere or the Hallett Nature Sanctuary edge. The east and north sides are your quiet zones while the afternoon music plays mid-park.

## Mon 6/22 — Rain-quiet gardens + an evening prayer service

Showers and thunderstorms, 77°. The Conservatory Garden in light rain is one of the most peaceful places in the city — bring a hood and have it to yourself. In the evening, a **Shoresh Prayer Service gathers at The Pool (6 PM)** for anyone drawn to a contemplative, spiritual moment in the park's quiet north end.

## Tue 6/23 — Soft, wet, solitary

Rain showers likely, 80°. A second still day — the Ramble, North Woods, and the Loch hold quiet and cover. A slow, unhurried walk is the whole practice today; let the weather thin the crowds for you.

## Wed 6/24 — Clear light, golden morning

84° and sunny — the standout. A dawn practice at Summit Rock or a slow loop through the Shakespeare and Conservatory Gardens rewards you with clear, golden light. Get there at opening for the garden in stillness before the day warms.

## Thu 6/25 — Find the empty side

80° with a chance of storms. With league play heaviest on the west and central fields today, the **east side and the north woods are your calm** — Conservatory Garden, the Ravine, Hallett. Time your visit to the dry windows.

## Fri 6/26 — Gentle close

83° with scattered showers and no programming. A soft self-guided morning by the Reservoir or in the North Woods — the light off the water is lovely even under broken clouds, and the week's quiet is yours to keep.

## Quick recap

- **Sun 6/21 is the solstice** — longest light of the year; bookend the day with a dawn sit and a late-light evening walk.
- **Mon 6/22, 6 PM:** Shoresh Prayer Service at The Pool — the week's one spiritual gathering.
- **Mon–Tue rain = empty gardens** — Conservatory Garden and the Ramble at their most peaceful; go early.
- **Wed 6/24** — clear and golden, the dawn-practice day.
- **For solitude all week:** north end and east side stay calm while music and markets cluster mid-park and at the southwest gates.

Be well,
