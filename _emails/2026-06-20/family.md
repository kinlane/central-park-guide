# Central Park Family Plans — Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26

*Quick note: this edition usually lands Friday night — I let it slip a day again, so here it is Saturday morning, covering Sat Jun 20 – Fri Jun 26.*

Hi families — the weekend is stacked with free, kid-friendly things: storytelling, model sailboats, chess for beginners, and a citywide make-your-own-music day on Sunday. Then a rainy Monday–Tuesday is your cue for indoor backup before a perfect Wednesday returns.

## Weather this week

Sunny and warm for the weekend (Sat 84°, Sun 83° with a small chance of an afternoon storm), then showers and thunderstorms Mon–Tue (77°/80°), a clear and beautiful 84° Wednesday, and warm, on-and-off-showery Thu–Fri (80°/83°). Plan the big outdoor outings for Sat, Sun morning, and Wednesday; keep a rainy-day plan ready for Mon–Tue.

## Sat 6/20 — Storytelling, model sailboats, and beginner chess

A banner morning. **Storytelling in Central Park at the Hans Christian Andersen statue (10 AM)** is the classic — bring a blanket. Across the path, **Model Yacht Racing at Kerbs Boathouse on Conservatory Water (9 AM)** is mesmerizing for little kids, and the **Chess Lecture & Beginners Workshop at the Chess & Checkers House (9 AM)** is great for older ones. Heads-up: the East Meadow is closed all weekend, so pick another lawn for a picnic.

## Sun 6/21 — Make Music New York (Father's Day)

83°, sunny early with a chance of an afternoon storm. **Make Music New York** brings free, all-ages performances to **Summit Rock, Pilgrim Hill, and the Dana Discovery Center at 3 PM** — wander between them. Earlier, **international folk dancers at King Jagiello Plaza (11 AM)** are a fun, low-key watch near the Turtle Pond. A great Father's Day stretch if you go before the afternoon clouds build.

## Mon 6/22 — Rainy day, go to your backup

Showers and thunderstorms, 77°. Not a playground day. If you're set on the park, the **"40 in Focus" SummerStage photo exhibit at the Arsenal Gallery** is free and indoors. Otherwise this is a museum / library afternoon.

## Tue 6/23 — Wet again, quiet park

Rain showers likely, 80°. Another indoor-leaning day — the Arsenal exhibit holds up, and the zoo is always a rainy-day standby. Save the energy for Wednesday.

## Wed 6/24 — The week's best park day, plus a kids' event

84° and sunny — get outside. **Rothy's Kids' Event at Pilgrim Hill (late afternoon)** is a fun, free-form draw if you're near the east side. Otherwise it's the day for the playgrounds, the carousel, and Conservatory Water. Bank the outdoor time while the sky's clear.

## Thu 6/25 — Warm, unsettled, market at Merchants' Gate

80° with a chance of storms. The **Columbus Circle food market at Merchants' Gate (9:30 AM)** is an easy snack-and-stroll at the southwest corner. Keep the day flexible and watch the sky.

## Fri 6/26 — Easy close to the week

83° with scattered showers and no big programming — playgrounds and the carousel between the raindrops. The food market is back at Merchants' Gate if you want a destination.

## Quick recap

- **Sat is the big one:** storytelling at Hans Christian Andersen (10 AM), model sailboats at Kerbs Boathouse (9 AM), beginner chess (9 AM).
- **Sun:** Make Music New York at Summit Rock, Pilgrim Hill, and Dana Discovery Center (3 PM) + folk dancing at King Jagiello (11 AM).
- **Wed 6/24 is the clear day** — playgrounds, carousel, and the Rothy's Kids' Event at Pilgrim Hill.
- **Mon–Tue are rainy** — Arsenal Gallery exhibit or the zoo for backup.
- **East Meadow closed all weekend** — pick another lawn for picnics.

Have a great week in the park,
— Central Park Guide
