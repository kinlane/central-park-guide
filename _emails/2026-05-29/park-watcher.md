# Central Park Guide for Park Watchers — Fri May 29 – Thu Jun 4

Hi park-watchers,

This is a transition week. The J.P. Morgan Corporate Challenge teardown on Friday closes out the spring-race calendar; on Saturday the SummerStage permit goes active at Rumsey even though the first headline show doesn't land until Jun 11; and the Naumburg Orchestral Concerts season — running since 1905 — opens Tue Jun 9. The shape of the week is the calendar pivoting from the spring-program tail-end into the early-summer rollout, with a long quiet stretch in between.

## Weather this week

Cool breezy weekend with a stunning Sunday, mild unsettled midweek with afternoon storm chances Mon–Tue, then a warming Thu with the first 80s of June. First hint of June heat building toward an 88°F Friday next week — worth flagging as the season's first heat wave on the horizon.

## Fri May 29 — Corporate Challenge teardown closes spring-race season

The two-night J.P. Morgan Corporate Challenge ran Wed and Thu evenings; Friday is staging strike and fencing removal at South Dead Road B and Bandshell Plaza through midday. This effectively closes the spring corporate-race calendar in the park. Columbus Circle Food Market is up at Merchants' Gate, and Julius Caesar (Part 1 of NY Classical Theatre's Barefoot Shakespeare production) opens its three-night run at The Pool Lawns.

## Sat May 30 — Five streams of programming on a single Saturday

Saturday is the week's busiest day by a wide margin, with five distinct activity streams overlapping:

- **CRCA Central Park Racing Series crit** on East Drive, 4–8:30 AM (the recurring weekly Saturday-AM crit — loop is clean by 9)
- **Seneca Village Photo Walk** at 11 AM, meeting at West 85th and CPW — a Conservancy-led walk through the site of the 19th-century African American community displaced for the park's construction
- **SummerStage permit activates** at Rumsey Playfield + Bandshell, 8 AM–11:59 PM — season opener / site activation, not a ticketed show
- **YAI Central Park Challenge** at Bandshell Plaza, 8 AM–noon — annual inclusive walk/run
- **Sing for Hope Choir Fest** at The Dairy, noon–3 PM — free community choir festival mid-park

That's the spring-program tail-end and the summer rollout sharing a single Saturday. Also on the calendar: Model Yacht Racing at Conservatory Water, Cast for Kids at The Pond, Skate Circle on Dead Road North, and a Spring Picnic on the Great Lawn's Southwest Lawn. East Meadow is closed for the weekend.

## Sun May 31 — Annual Picnic at Ross Pinetum

The Sunday standout is the **Annual Picnic at Ross Pinetum West** (1–4 PM) — flagged Annual Tradition on the permit and worth naming for that reason. The Pinetum sits on the west side around 105th, a quieter corner that gets its one signature gathering of the year. Recurring Sunday fixtures also fire: the Central Park International Folkdancers at King Jagiełło Plaza (11 AM–7 PM, weekly), and Skate Circle on Dead Road North (2–7 PM, weekly). Julius Caesar Part 1 closes its three-night run. East Meadow remains closed.

## Mon Jun 1 — CP Sports Summer Program opens

Summer youth-sport rollout begins. **CP Sports Summer Program** opens at South Dead Road A + B on a weekday split (8:30 AM–noon AM session, 12:30–4 PM PM session) and will run through the week. Not a large footprint, but it's the start of the recurring summer programming at South Dead Road that will run through August. East Meadow closed for its maintenance day completes the multi-day closure.

## Tue Jun 2 — Julius Caesar Part 2 opens

Quiet on the permit side. Barefoot Shakespeare's **Julius Caesar Part 2** opens its three-night run at The Pool Lawns (5–10 PM, Tue/Wed/Thu) — the back half of the two-part production. No other significant programming.

## Wed Jun 3 — Wild West Playground Lawn maintenance

Operational housekeeping day. **Wild West Playground Lawn** (west side mid-park) closed to 2 PM for maintenance. Otherwise a quiet midweek day; Julius Caesar Part 2 continues at The Pool.

## Thu Jun 4 — East 106 St South Mount maintenance

Another maintenance day: **East 106th St South Mount** (far north-east) closed to 2 PM. Columbus Circle Food Market is back at Merchants' Gate. Julius Caesar Part 2 closes its run. The warmest day of the week at 83°F is the first hint of the heat building toward next Friday's forecast 88°F.

## Quick recap

- **Spring-race calendar closes Friday** with Corporate Challenge teardown — no NYRR races in the park this week
- **Saturday stacks five streams of activity** — CRCA crit, Seneca Village Photo Walk, SummerStage season open, YAI Challenge, Sing for Hope Choir Fest
- **Annual Tradition flag:** Annual Picnic at Ross Pinetum West, Sun 6/1
- **Summer rollout begins Mon 6/1** with CP Sports Summer Program at South Dead Road
- **Two maintenance days midweek** — Wild West Playground Lawn Wed, East 106 St South Mount Thu
- **East Meadow closed Sat–Mon** — multi-day closure on the north-east stretch behind the Met
- **First-of-season firsts looking ahead:** Naumburg Orchestral Concerts opens Tue Jun 9 (since 1905); SummerStage first headline Thu Jun 11 (Yellowcard / New Found Glory / Plain White T's)
- **Weather pattern:** first 80s of June on Thursday, with the season's first heat wave building toward an 88°F Friday next week

One closing aside on the longer arc: NYC Marathon training season (18-week plan) starts the week of Mon Jun 29 — about four weeks out. The loop's footfall will start picking up noticeably then.

From the meadows,

— Central Park Guide
