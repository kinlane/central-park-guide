# Central Park Nature Notes: Mon Jun 29 – Sun Jul 5

Hi nature lovers,

No scheduled walks on the calendar this week, so this is a quiet-park, watch-the-weather edition — and the weather is the whole story. A heat wave reshapes where the life in the park goes, and where you'll want to be to find it.

## Weather this week

A building heat wave: 96° Wed, 100° Thu, 101° Fri, easing slightly over a stormy Fourth. Be in the park at dawn and stay in the canopy — the heat pushes everything toward water and shade, which is exactly where the watching is best.

## Mon Jun 29 & Tue Jun 30 — dawn chorus, then into the shade

The coolest mornings of the week and your best dawn-chorus windows. Get to the **Ramble** at first light, then follow the shade north to the **Loch** and **North Woods**, where the running water keeps the air ten degrees kinder. **Turtle Pond** turtles will be hauled out and basking early before retreating to the water as the day heats.

## Wed Jul 1 – Fri Jul 3 — the heat reshapes the park

As the temperature climbs into the triple digits, the open lawns empty of both people and birds, and life concentrates at the water. Walk the **Reservoir** at dawn for waterbirds, or sit quietly at **Hallett Nature Sanctuary** and the **Pond** — herons and egrets work the shallows in heat like this. The **Conservatory Garden** is in full midsummer bloom; go early, before it bakes.

## Sat Jul 4 & Sun Jul 5 — head north for quiet

The holiday packs the southern lawns with picnics, so the wild north end is your refuge: the **North Woods, the Loch, and the Ravine** stay green, shaded, and largely empty even on the Fourth. Sunday's storms may break the heat and bring out the waterfalls along the Loch at their fullest — a good post-rain walk if the timing works.

## Quick recap

**Best windows:** dawn, every day — the only cool, quiet hours this week.

**Mon–Tue:** strongest dawn chorus; Ramble early, then the Loch and North Woods.

**Wed–Fri (96–101°):** life concentrates at water — Reservoir, Hallett, the Pond, Turtle Pond.

**Conservatory Garden:** peak midsummer bloom — go early.

**Holiday weekend:** escape the crowds in the wild north end; watch for full Loch waterfalls after Sunday's storms.

In the green and the shade,
The Central Park Guide
