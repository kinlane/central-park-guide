# Central Park Sports: Mon Jun 29 – Sun Jul 5

Hey sports fans,

No races on the loop this holiday week, but the fields are busy — at least until the heat wave clears them out midweek. Here's the diamond-by-diamond read.

## Weather this week

A heat wave builds fast — 96° Wed, 100° Thu, 101° Fri, 97° on a stormy Fourth. Morning games are fine early in the week; from Wednesday on, anything past mid-morning is heat-risk territory, and you can see leagues backing off in the permit counts.

## Mon Jun 29 & Tue Jun 30 — peak field week

The busiest stretch by far. **Tuesday alone has 67 field permits** — 27 at **North Meadow**, 22 on the **Great Lawn**, 16 at **Heckscher** — softball, baseball, and youth leagues stacked through the morning and evening. Monday's nearly as full (53). If you're playing or watching, these two mornings are the action; get there before the heat.

## Wed Jul 1 – Fri Jul 3 — the heat thins the slate

Permits drop sharply as the temperature spikes: **29 Wednesday, 29 Thursday, 21 Friday**, concentrated at North Meadow and Heckscher in the cooler early hours. Expect cancellations and walk-offs once it hits the mid-90s — play early or not at all. This is a hydrate-between-innings week.

## Sat Jul 4 & Sun Jul 5 — holiday games return

Field play bounces back over the holiday — **34 Saturday, 36 Sunday**, again led by North Meadow and the Great Lawn — but the Fourth is 97° with afternoon thunderstorms, so morning slots are the safe ones. **Model yachting** runs on Conservatory Water at Kerbs Boathouse both weekend mornings if you want the calmer end of the sports spectrum.

## Quick recap

**Mon–Tue:** peak field week — 53 then 67 permits, North Meadow / Great Lawn / Heckscher.

**Wed–Fri:** heat thins the slate to ~21–29/day; play early, expect cancellations.

**Sat 7/4 & Sun:** games rebound (34 / 36) but a 97° stormy holiday — morning only.

**Weekend mornings:** model yachting on Conservatory Water.

**No races on the loop this week.**

Play early, hydrate hard,
The Central Park Guide
