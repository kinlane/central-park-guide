Hi nature lovers — the week's real story isn't on the events calendar. A 98°F heat wave has broken into a mild, wet stretch, and two days of thunderstorms mid-week will do more for the park's water features and its animals than anything scheduled.

## Weather this week
Highs settle at 79–85°F with mid-60s nights, and Tuesday and Wednesday both bring showers and thunderstorms before Thursday clears into a dry, sunny weekend. Thursday and Friday mornings are the ones to take off work for — after a real soaking, in cool air, with the crowds still thin, the Ravine and the North Woods will be as good as they get in high summer.

## Mon Jul 20 — clear and still, before the rain
Sunny, 80°F, and a 1 mph wind, which is close to dead calm. Still air means Turtle Pond and the Lake go glassy, and the reflections off Belvedere are worth an early trip. Late July is a quiet stretch for birds — breeding season is winding down, fall migration hasn't started, and what you'll mostly find is family groups: fledged robins and cardinals still begging, wood ducks and mallards with half-grown broods on the Lake and the Meer. Green herons are reliable at the Pond and at Turtle Pond edges this time of year, hunting the shallows at first light.

## Tue Jul 21 and Wed Jul 22 — two days of storms, and what they do
Thunderstorms both days. If you're willing to get wet, go — this is the most interesting the park gets.

- **The Loch and the Ravine** only really run after heavy rain. The waterfalls in the North Woods go from trickle to genuine sound, and by Wednesday afternoon the whole ravine will be loud and cool and empty of people.
- **Rain brings everything down to eye level.** Birds drop out of the canopy to bathe and forage in puddles; the Ramble's stream edges and the drip lines under the big oaks are where to stand.
- **After a storm passes**, the twenty minutes of low light and steam coming off warm stone at Belvedere and along the Gill is the best photography of the week.

Watch for lightning and stay out of the open lawns and off the high ground at Summit Rock and the Great Hill when cells are moving through.

## Thu Jul 23 — the pick of the week
Clearing, 80°F, light northerly wind. The park will be washed clean, the air will be cool, and the crowds haven't returned yet. This is a Ramble morning: get in by 7, work the Gill from the Azalea Pond down, and give it two slow hours. Dragonflies over Turtle Pond will be active as it warms toward midday — high summer is peak odonate season, and the Pond's east edge is the place for it.

## Fri Jul 24 — quiet, and the Conservatory Garden at its peak
Clearing to sunny and the lightest day on the calendar. The **Conservatory Garden** is in full high-summer bloom right now and is genuinely close to empty before 9 AM — the north Italianate garden with its perennial borders is the one to sit in. Hallett Nature Sanctuary is the other place worth your morning if you want somewhere the park forgets about.

There's a **cyanotype T-shirt printing workshop on the Great Hill from 9 AM** — sun-printing, which is a small piece of botany-adjacent craft if that appeals.

## Sat Jul 25 — warm, sunny, and full of people
85°F and the first pleasant Saturday in weeks, so the park will be at capacity. Go north. The **North Woods, the Loch, and the Blockhouse** absorb crowds better than anywhere else, and the Meer's north shore stays quiet even on a busy day. The East Meadow is closed all weekend. If you want the Ramble on a Saturday, it has to be before 8.

## Sun Jul 26 — the Harlem Meer
**Summer on the Harlem Meer at the Dana Discovery Center from 9 AM** is the week's one genuine nature-adjacent program — the Meer is the best place in the park to watch a summer morning, with herons, egrets, turtles, and the catch-and-release fishing crowd all working the same water. There's a large running race on the drives from 7:30 to 10, which is a reason to walk in from the north rather than through the middle of the park.

## A note on what's missing
There are no Conservancy walking tours or guided bird walks in this week's listings. That's a gap in our sources rather than an empty park — the Conservancy's tour booking system and the bird-walk schedules aren't currently feeding our calendar, and we're working on it. If you want a guided walk this week, check centralparknyc.org and the NYC Bird Alliance directly rather than trusting our silence.

## Quick recap
- **Thu and Fri mornings are the week** — post-storm, cool, and quiet. Ramble on Thursday, Conservatory Garden on Friday.
- **Tue and Wed storms make the Loch and the Ravine run.** Go in the rain if you can.
- **Late July means family groups, not migration** — fledglings, duck broods, green herons at Turtle Pond and the Pond.
- **Sunday — Summer on the Harlem Meer**, 9 AM at the Dana Discovery Center.
- **Saturday is crowded.** North Woods and the Meer's north shore, or go before 8.
- **No guided walks in our data this week** — a source gap, not an empty calendar.
