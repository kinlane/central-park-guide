Hi park watchers — this is a transitional week. The month-long FIFA activation finally clears the Great Lawn, the permit load stays at its summer ceiling, and Sunday brings the largest single running field the park has hosted since the June race weekend.

## Weather this week
The heat wave that peaked at 98°F last Wednesday has broken decisively — highs run 79–85°F all week, with showers and thunderstorms Tuesday and Wednesday before a dry, sunny weekend. Worth logging as a pattern: this is the first full week since late June without a heat advisory shaping the park's usage, and you'll see it in how the lawns fill.

## The Great Lawn is handed back
The **World Cup Final Viewing Experience** runs its last mornings Monday and Tuesday and then the Great Lawn comes out of a long occupation. Between the FIFA Arena activation, the bus operations at Columbus Circle, and the final viewing setup, the park's largest open lawn has been substantially unavailable through the tournament. Two things to watch: how quickly the turf is released back to general use, and what condition it's in. A hot, heavily-trafficked July on the Great Lawn usually means a restoration closure follows — if one is coming, it'll appear in the permit data within a fortnight.

## NYRR Team Championships 5M — Sun Jul 26, 7:30 AM
Roughly 5,000 club runners, starting and finishing on the **102nd Street Cross Drive**, west onto West Drive and around the lower loop. Men's, women's, and non-binary points races, worth double points in the 2026 Club Points Series. Race-Day Central sits behind the backstop of Ballfield #8 near East Drive and 102nd, with bib pickup and bag check from 6 AM. The drives carry race traffic roughly 7–10 AM.

**The provenance here is worth your attention.** This race appeared in none of our automated feeds. NYRR's site sits behind a Queue-It waiting room that blocks scripted access, and the NYC Open Data permit feed doesn't carry NYRR events at all — so the largest loop-affecting event of the week was invisible until it was hand-seeded this weekend. That's now the second consecutive category of major event (after the spring charity walks) that only reaches the calendar through manual curation. It's a structural gap in public data about the park, not a one-off.

## Closures on the books
- **Bethesda Terrace and Fountain** — listed closed the full week. The park's single most-visited architectural feature, offline for seven days, with no stated reason in the permit record.
- **East Meadow** — closed Saturday and Sunday.
- **Great Lawn** — effectively unavailable Monday and Tuesday, then released.

## Permit load
The weekday permit count sits at its summer ceiling and is holding: roughly 57 sport permits Monday, 64 Tuesday, 61 Wednesday, and a peak of **75 Thursday** — the heaviest single day in recent weeks. Friday drops sharply to 41, then the weekend runs 53 Saturday and 67 Sunday. Total curated calendar for the week is about 629 events across all sources.

The composition is unchanged from the last month: youth summer programming (SSS sessions at West 96th Street Landscape, the East Meadow, and Rudin Playground Lawn; James Christie soccer training; CP Sports at the South Dead Road) plus the standing softball and baseball league blocks. Private bookings continue at their usual mid-summer rate — weddings and ceremonies clustered at Cop Cot, Ladies' Pavilion, and the Conservatory Garden, nothing individually notable.

## New and recurring on the calendar
- The **Columbus Circle Food Market** at Merchants' Gate posts a three-day run this week, Thursday through Saturday — worth watching whether that becomes a standing pattern or is a one-week expansion.
- **Naumburg's 121st season** continues Tuesday with Orchestra of St. Luke's; two concerts remain after this one.
- **SummerStage** brings a significant dance program to Rumsey Wednesday — Complexions, Paul Taylor, and LaTasha. Note the previously scheduled Anjunadeep show at Rumsey is carrying a **POSTPONED** flag in the source data, and the Yiddish program has moved to September.
- **"40 in Focus"** at the Arsenal, the SummerStage 40th-anniversary photography retrospective, continues weekday mornings.

## Data notes for the record
Two sources are silently contributing nothing this week. The **Public Theater season file is empty**, so no Delacorte performance dates are in the calendar despite the Shakespeare in the Park season being live — it surfaces in the centralpark.com feed but not as scheduled events. The **bird walk seed files are also empty**, so no NYC Bird Alliance or Birding Bob walks appear. Separately, the Conservancy and centralpark.com caches are being fetched but aren't consumed by the merge, so those events are stale rather than current. None of these are new failures, but all three suppress real programming from the calendar.
