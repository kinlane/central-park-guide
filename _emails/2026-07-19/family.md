Hi families — the heat wave is over, which changes everything about taking kids to the park this week. Low 80s instead of upper 90s, free learn-to-swim sessions Tuesday, and a Saturday with more going on than any single family can actually do.

## Weather this week
Highs of 79–85°F all week with cool mid-60s nights, but Tuesday and Wednesday bring showers and thunderstorms before it clears Thursday into a sunny weekend. Monday and the weekend are the outdoor days; keep Tuesday and Wednesday flexible and have an indoor fallback ready, because the storms this week are the pop-up kind that arrive fast.

## Mon Jul 20 — the good-weather day, and the Great Lawn is still fenced
Sunny and 80°F. Worth knowing before you head over: the Great Lawn is closed off for the last day of the World Cup Final viewing setup, so if that's your usual open-space destination, pick the North Meadow or Sheep Meadow instead. **Gottesman Pool at the Davis Center has free lap swim from 7 AM** — and the summer sports programs are running all day at the South Dead Road and the East 102nd Street Clay Oval if you have a kid enrolled.

## Tue Jul 21 — free learn-to-swim at Gottesman Pool
The best family value of the week, and it's free. **Parent and Tots Learn to Swim at 10 AM and 11 AM**, and **Children's Learn to Swim at 1 PM**, all at Gottesman Pool at the Davis Center. Storms are in the forecast, so check before you go — but a pool session is one of the few plans that survives a rainy day intact. The SSS summer sessions and soccer training run their usual weekday slots.

## Wed Jul 22 — a rainy-day plan
Thunderstorms and 82°F. Not a park day for small kids unless you're committed. If you want something free and indoors nearby, the **"40 in Focus" SummerStage photography exhibit at the Arsenal** is open from 9 AM — it's one room, it's free, and forty years of concert photographs will hold an eight-year-old for about twenty minutes, which is exactly as long as you need it to.

## Thu Jul 23 — clearing, plus a free concert on the Great Lawn
Slight chance of showers, 80°F, and much better. The **Columbus Circle Food Market** opens at Merchants' Gate from 9:30 AM — easy lunch if you're coming in from the south. In the evening there's a **free concert on the Great Lawn at 7 PM**; the lawn fills from late afternoon, and if your kids can handle a late night, spreading a blanket at a free outdoor concert is a core Central Park summer memory. Go early to get space.

## Fri Jul 24 — cyanotype T-shirt printing at the Great Hill
A genuinely great one: a **cyanotype T-shirt printing workshop at the Great Hill from 9 AM**. Sun-printing on fabric is exactly the kind of craft that works outdoors, it's hands-on, and the results come home wearable. Clearing to sunny, and the lightest day of the week otherwise — a good day to add a playground and not rush.

## Sat Jul 25 — the biggest day of the week
85°F and sunny. Start early and pick two or three, not all of them:

- **Model yacht racing at Kerbs Boathouse on Conservatory Water, 9 AM** — the remote-controlled sailboats, always a hit
- **Chess lecture and beginners workshop at the Chess and Checkers House, 9 AM** — good for a kid who's just learning
- **Tot Shabbat at Levin Playground, 9 AM**
- **Storytelling at the Hans Christian Andersen Statue, 10 AM** — the statue is right by Conservatory Water, so this pairs perfectly with the yachts
- **That Guitar Man From Central Park at Hernshead, 12 PM**
- **Skate Circle at Dead Road North, 2 PM** — fun to watch even if nobody in your family skates

The **East Meadow is closed all weekend**. The Conservatory Water cluster — yachts at 9, storytelling at 10 — is the single best-value morning of the week if you want one plan that carries two hours.

## Sun Jul 26 — the north end, and a race in the morning
There's a large running race on the park drives from 7:30 to about 10 AM, which mostly just means the loop is busy early — not a factor if you're arriving later. **Summer on the Harlem Meer at the Dana Discovery Center from 9 AM** is the day's main family event and worth the trip north. Then the **International Folkdancers at King Jagiello Plaza at 11**, a **Steppers dance demo at Summit Rock at 1:30**, and the **Skate Circle again at 2**.

## Quick recap
- **Tue — free learn-to-swim at Gottesman Pool**, 10 and 11 AM for tots, 1 PM for children.
- **Fri — cyanotype T-shirt workshop at the Great Hill**, 9 AM.
- **Sat morning — model yachts at 9, storytelling at 10**, both at Conservatory Water. Best pairing of the week.
- **Thu 7 PM — free concert on the Great Lawn.** Go early for space.
- **Sun — Summer on the Harlem Meer** from 9 AM at the Dana Discovery Center.
- **Great Lawn fenced Monday; East Meadow closed all weekend.**

See you out there,
The Central Park Guide
