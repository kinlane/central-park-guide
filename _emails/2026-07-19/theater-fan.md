Hi theater fans — one enormous night this week. Wednesday, SummerStage puts Complexions Contemporary Ballet, the Paul Taylor Dance Company, and LaTasha on the same free bill at Rumsey Playfield. That is a program you would pay Lincoln Center prices for, and it costs nothing.

## Weather this week
The heat has broken into a mild 79–85°F week, but Tuesday and Wednesday both carry showers and thunderstorms before Thursday clears into a dry weekend. Wednesday's storm risk lands squarely on the big night — SummerStage holds and delays rather than cancelling when it's marginal, so the move is to show up prepared to wait rather than to stay home reading the radar.

## Wed Jul 22, 8 PM — Complexions / Paul Taylor / LaTasha, Rumsey Playfield, free
The week, in one evening.

**Paul Taylor Dance Company** is one of the foundational American modern dance companies — Taylor came out of Martha Graham's company and Merce Cunningham's orbit and spent sixty years building a repertory that is, more than anything, musical. Seeing them outdoors on a summer night, free, is a genuinely different experience from seeing them in a proscenium house: you lose the sightlines and gain the sky.

**Complexions Contemporary Ballet**, founded by two Ailey alumni, is the athletic counterweight — ballet technique driven through contemporary and popular music, and a company that reads extremely well to an outdoor crowd that isn't necessarily a dance audience. **LaTasha** rounds out the bill.

Practical: the queue at the 72nd Street entrance starts forming around 6:30 for an 8 PM curtain. Free but first-come, and this one will draw. Get in line by 7 at the latest. Bring a poncho rather than an umbrella — you'll be asked to put an umbrella down.

## Mon Jul 20, 5 PM — Dance Is Life at the Bandshell
A gentler opener on the best-weather day of the week. **Dance Is Life** at Bandshell Plaza at 5 PM, on a sunny, still, 80°F evening. The Bandshell is a more intimate space than Rumsey and worth walking the Mall to reach.

## Sat Jul 25, 6 PM — tango at the Shakespeare Statue
Social tango in the open air at the south end of the Mall, at golden hour, on an 85°F sunny evening. Not a production, but performance in the older sense — and if you've never watched the Shakespeare Statue tango, it's one of the park's most quietly theatrical recurring scenes. There's also a **Bandshell concert at 2 PM** earlier that afternoon.

## Sun Jul 26, 1:30 PM — Steppers at Summit Rock
A **Steppers performance and dance demo at Summit Rock**. Stepping is a percussive, highly disciplined tradition with deep roots in Black fraternity and sorority culture — it rewards close watching, and Summit Rock, the highest point in the park, is an unusual and good venue for it. The **International Folkdancers** are at King Jagiello Plaza from 11 AM if you want a participatory morning first.

## Weekdays — "40 in Focus" at the Arsenal
Free, from 9 AM on weekdays: **"40 in Focus: SummerStage through the Lens of Photographer Jack Vartoogian"**, forty years of the series photographed from the pit. Go on Wednesday afternoon before the show — seeing four decades of that stage and then standing in front of it the same night is the best programming accident of the week.
