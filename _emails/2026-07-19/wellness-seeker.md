Hi — the single best thing about this week isn't an event. The heat wave broke, and for the first time in weeks you can be outside at dawn without it being an endurance exercise. There's also a free midday meditation gathering at the Bandshell on Friday, which is the best-matched thing on this calendar in a long while.

## Weather this week
Highs of 79–85°F and mid-60s mornings replace last week's 98°F, though Tuesday and Wednesday bring thunderstorms before Thursday clears into a dry, sunny weekend. Rebuild a morning practice this week while the air is cool — Thursday and Friday at sunrise, after the storms have scrubbed the humidity out, will be the two best mornings the park has offered since June.

## Fri Jul 24, 12 PM — Meditation Marathon: Peace in the Park
**The week's event.** A free midday meditation gathering at **Bandshell Plaza**, on a clearing, sunny day — and Friday is the quietest day on the park's entire calendar this week, with barely anything else permitted anywhere. A group sit in the open air at the Bandshell, with the Mall's elms overhead, on the emptiest day of the week, is about as good a setup as this park offers.

If group practice isn't your thing, take the Friday quiet anyway. It's the day to go find somewhere and stay there.

## Every morning, 7 AM — free lap swim at Gottesman Pool
Worth flagging now that the weather has turned: **Gottesman Pool at the Davis Center runs free lap swim from 7 AM daily.** Free, outdoors, first thing, no booking. Swimming is the one form of movement that stays pleasant regardless of what the weather does midweek, which makes it the most reliable thing on this list.

## Mon Jul 20 — still air, and the Reservoir at sunrise
Sunny, 80°F, and a 1 mph wind — effectively dead calm. Still mornings are when the Reservoir goes to glass and the whole north basin holds the skyline upside down. Sunrise is around 5:45; be on the track by 6 and you'll have it nearly to yourself. Mid-park is busier than usual — the Great Lawn is fenced for the last day of the World Cup setup — so stay north or west.

## Tue Jul 21 and Wed Jul 22 — storms, and where to be indoors-adjacent
Thunderstorms both days. Two options. Practice early, before the cells build — mornings are largely clear both days. Or lean into it: the **Ravine and the North Woods** during and just after rain are the most genuinely solitary the park ever gets, and the sound of the Loch actually running drowns out the city in a way nothing else in Manhattan does.

Avoid the high open ground — Summit Rock and the Great Hill — when there's lightning about.

## Thu Jul 23 — the best morning of the week
Clearing, cool, light northerly wind, and the air washed clean by two days of rain. Take this one. The **Conservatory Garden** opens early and is close to empty before 9 — the north garden's perennial borders are at their high-summer peak and there are benches nobody sits on. **Hallett Nature Sanctuary** is the other choice if you want somewhere the park genuinely forgets exists.

Note there's a large free concert on the Great Lawn at 7 PM; the mid-park fills from late afternoon, so keep the evening plans north or west of it.

## Sat Jul 25 — a crowded day, and where to disappear on it
85°F, sunny, and the first properly nice Saturday in weeks — meaning the park will be at full capacity from about 10 AM. Where to find solitude: **the North Woods, the Blockhouse, and the Meer's north shore** absorb crowds better than anywhere else in the park, and the **Ross Pinetum** stays quiet even on a busy weekend. Anywhere south of 72nd Street is a lost cause after mid-morning. The East Meadow is closed all weekend.

## Sun Jul 26, 4 PM — Isha Foundation volunteer meet-up at the Great Hill
A gathering at the **Great Hill at 4 PM** if community practice appeals. Earlier in the day there's a large running race on the drives from 7:30 to 10 — mostly a reason to keep your morning off the loop and in the interior. **Summit Rock**, quiet and high and well away from the race route, is the pick for a Sunday morning sit.

## Quick recap
- **Fri 12 PM — Peace in the Park meditation at the Bandshell**, free, on the week's quietest day.
- **Free lap swim at Gottesman Pool, 7 AM daily** — the most weather-proof thing on the calendar.
- **Thursday sunrise is the best morning of the week.** Conservatory Garden before 9.
- **Monday is dead calm** — the Reservoir at 6 AM.
- **Tue and Wed rain makes the Ravine and the Loch** the most solitary places in Manhattan.
- **Saturday is crowded** — go north of 96th or don't go after 10.

Take the cool mornings while they last,
The Central Park Guide
