# Central Park Wellness Week: Fri May 22 – Thu May 28

Hi there,

A thin week on the wellness calendar with one quietly contemplative offering — **Mindtravel Silent Piano at the Bandshell Friday at 7 PM**, a headphone-piano experience that sits closer to a sound bath than a concert. Beyond that, the week is yours to shape: **Tuesday dawn is the calmest, driest window for outdoor practice**, and the weekend storms make Saturday and Sunday indoor days.

## Weather this week

Cool and showery weekend with **rain all day Saturday and thunderstorms likely Sunday**, lingering rain showers on a 74°F Memorial Day, then a Tuesday dry-out (80°F mostly cloudy) and an 85°F partly-sunny Wednesday. **Tue dawn is your outdoor-practice window of the week**; weekend storms favor indoor practice, and the quiet northern zones (Hallett, Ramble, North Woods, Summit Rock) are unbooked all week.

## Fri May 22 — Mindtravel Silent Piano at the Bandshell, 7 PM

The week's one wellness-adjacent event. **Mindtravel** is a silent-disco-style piano performance — you wear wireless headphones, the pianist plays a long ambient set, and a couple hundred people sit on the grass at Bandshell Plaza listening to the same thing without a speaker in earshot. Closer to a sound bath than a concert. Free, weather-permitting, doors-open feel — arrive 6:30 PM with a blanket. If the rain breaks, this is the move.

## Sat May 23 — rain all day, indoor practice day

58°F and steady rain. The Bandshell, Hernshead, Skate Circle, and Salsa Social will all run anyway — **avoid the south end entirely**. Today is for your home mat, a studio class, or a slow rainy walk through the Ramble if you have the gear and want the silence. The Ramble in steady rain is one of the park's most contemplative spaces; the canopy mutes the city to nearly nothing.

## Sun May 24 — thunderstorms, no outdoor practice

62°F with storms building through the afternoon. **No outdoor meditation today** — lightning risk over the Reservoir and the open lawns is real. Save the practice for indoors. If you need to be in the park, a short morning walk before the storms arrive (8–10 AM window) is the only safe outdoor option.

## Mon May 25 — Memorial Day showers, head north for quiet

74°F with scattered rain showers, and the south end runs noisy with skate circles and private events. **The northern third of the park is wide open all week** — no permits in Hallett, the Ramble, North Woods, or Summit Rock. A late-morning sit at **Summit Rock** (the park's highest natural point, at 83rd on the west side) catches you between showers and gives you the season's most underused view.

## Tue May 26 — the dawn window, 5:30–7 AM

This is the morning to set the alarm for. Post-rain air, 80°F day so the dawn will be cool, mostly cloudy so the light is soft. **5:30 AM at Conservatory Garden** — the gates open early, the formal walled garden is empty, the iris and allium are coming on as the late peonies fade. Or **6 AM in the Ramble** — bird song fully back, the woods drying out, paths to yourself. Pick one. 30 minutes is enough.

## Wed May 27 — 85°F, dawn or shade only

The warmest day of the year so far. **Dawn practice before 7 AM** or you're committed to canopy walks. Hallett Nature Sanctuary is the coolest spot in the south park; the North Woods is the coolest spot anywhere. Skip the loop in the evening — it'll be busy with a corporate run-and-finish crowd. Stay north of 86th Street and you won't notice.

## Thu May 28 — Best outdoor practice morning of the week

80°F sunny and dry — **this is the cleanest outdoor-practice weather of the entire week**, even better than Tuesday's dawn. Hallett, the Ramble, the North Woods, and Summit Rock are all wide open in the morning (Summit Rock has the **Barefoot Shakespeare opening at 3 PM**, so go early if you want the rock to yourself). **The Pond Lawn is closed for maintenance all day** — a small loss, but the Hallett walks are still open. Don't conflict with the Corporate Challenge wind-down on the south loop; the rest of the park is yours.

## Where to find quiet any day this week

- **Hallett Nature Sanctuary** — unbooked all week, coolest south-park option
- **The Ramble** — heaviest canopy, bird song into evenings
- **North Woods & the Ravine** — waterfalls at full flow Mon onward
- **Summit Rock** — high point, low foot traffic, no permits
- **Conservatory Garden** — formal walled garden, empty at dawn
