# Central Park Sports This Week: Fri May 22 – Thu May 28

Hey sports fans,

**The J.P. Morgan Corporate Challenge owns Wednesday evening** — ~15,000 runners, 3.5 miles on the loop, roughly 6–9 PM — and it's the best spectator event in the park this month. Stake out a spot near the South Dead Road finish or anywhere along the West Drive grind. Around that, you've got Memorial Day field play across Heckscher / North Meadow / Great Lawn, Saturday Model Yacht Racing (rain pending), and the season's **first Salsa Dura social at the Bandshell Saturday evening** — the Bandshell music calendar opens for real.

## Weather this week

Cool and wet through the weekend — **Sat full rain, Sun thunderstorms** — warming Memorial Day with showers, dry Tuesday, and **Wednesday hitting 85°F**, the warmest of the week and the day the Corporate Challenge runs. Saturday Model Yacht Racing is on rain-cancel watch; Memorial Day league play happens unless lightning fires; Wed race-day heat means hydration and shade for spectators camping on the loop.

## Fri May 22 — full weekday roster, Field Day at Grand Army

Cool, possible AM showers, clearing later. **~110 youth + ~28 adult sports permits across the park** — biggest weekday roster of the week. **Field Day at Grand Army Plaza 10 AM** is the noticeable spectator item (school-organized, fun to walk past). **James Christie Soccer Training** continues at South Dead Road and Levin Playground — recurring weekday clinic. **Bowling Greens are closed for maintenance all day** — Bowling Green Lawn is dark, league bowlers will need to flex.

## Sat May 23 — Model Yachts on rain-watch, Salsa Dura opens the Bandshell

58°F and rain all day. **Model Yacht Racing at Kerbs Boathouse 9 AM–3 PM** is on the calendar but cancels in steady rain — Central Park Model Yacht Club's call, watch their feed in the morning. **~50 youth + ~31 adult permits across the fields** but the rain will scrub most of it; expect Heckscher and North Meadow to be a mud pit by noon. **East Meadow is closed** all day. **Central Park Skate Circle at Dead Road North 2–7 PM** — also rain-dependent, but the regulars sometimes go anyway. **Headline spectator pick if the rain breaks:** Salsa Dura Social at Bandshell 5–7:30 PM — first Bandshell music event of the season's social schedule, free, and worth a stop even if you're walking from a rained-out morning at Heckscher.

**Note:** the NYRR Open Run on Sat 8 AM tagged "Great Lawn" in the data is actually the **Astoria Park** Open Run in Queens — not a Central Park spectator event.

## Sun May 24 — Folkdancers, Skate Circle if storms hold off

62°F with thunderstorms likely. **~18 youth + ~25 adult permits** — a lighter Sunday slate, and most of it will scrub if the storms hit on schedule. **Central Park International Folkdancers at King Jagiełło 11 AM–7 PM** is the day's free social event — folk circles around the Władysław Jagiełło statue, weather-dependent but they often dance through drizzle. **Skate Circle** continues at Dead Road North 2–7 PM if dry. **East Meadow closed.** Plan around the radar.

## Mon May 25 (Memorial Day) — Corporate Challenge staging, ~74 youth + ~41 adult permits

74°F, rain showers likely. **Corporate Challenge setup begins at South Dead Road and Bandshell Plaza all day** — fencing, finish-line truss, runners-village build-out. **Memorial Day field play runs in force** — ~74 youth and ~41 adult permits, the biggest holiday-Monday roster you'll see, including the usual Heckscher softball stack and North Meadow Little League. Heat-rule modifications unlikely at 74°F but rain may shorten evening games. **East 102 St Landscape is closed for maintenance all day** — clay-oval-adjacent footprint.

## Tue May 26 — heavy weekday slate, Corporate Challenge build-out deepens

80°F, mostly cloudy, dry. **~93 youth + ~47 adult permits** — the heaviest weekday sports day of the week. Normal weekday softball stack at Heckscher and North Meadow; usual Little League at Great Lawn and Heckscher. **Corporate Challenge staging deepens at South Dead Road** — expo and runners-village fence-line going up, finish chute taking final shape. **Small Gathering in the Park at Heckscher Playground 4 PM** is a community drop-in adjacent to the fields. **East Meadow closed for maintenance.** Solid weekday spectate top to bottom.

## Wed May 27 — Corporate Challenge race day

**85°F partly sunny, race day.** The headline of the week. ~15,000 runners on a 3.5-mile loop course, evening start roughly 6–9 PM — corporate teams from across the city in matching shirts, finish at South Dead Road, post-race party at the Bandshell. **Stake out a spot on West Drive between 72nd and the Bandshell** for the best mix of pace-group action and finish-chute energy. Bring water — 85°F sun, no shade on the loop. **CP Sports Mini-Camp 9:30 AM at South Dead Road** runs in the morning before staging swallows the venue. **~86 youth + ~53 adult permits** — heavy field-play day, but Heckscher and North Meadow are the move because anything south-end is consumed by the race. **Julius Caesar at The Pool 10 AM** if you want a non-sports counter-program before the race (ongoing daily run, not closing).

## Thu May 28 — adult-league surge, ~57 adult permits is the week's high

**Thursday is the adult-league peak of the week — ~57 adult sports permits, the highest single-day adult count in the calendar**, with ~95 youth permits stacked on top. 80°F sunny means the fields are at full use: Heckscher and North Meadow softball stacks deepen through the evening, Great Lawn Little League runs its normal Thursday slate, and the post-work adult softball wave fills in everywhere by 6 PM. **Corporate Challenge Day 4 wraps the race series** — the permit's tagged Affects Loop, so expect light evening race activity on the south end winding down the multi-night format. **Non-sports-but-notable for the wandering fan:** **Barefoot Shakespeare's new June 2026 Show opens at Summit Rock 3 PM** — a fresh outdoor production at a rarely-used west-side venue, free, easy to swing by between innings.
