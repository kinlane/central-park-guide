# Central Park Wellness Week: Fri Jul 10 – Thu Jul 16

Hi wellness seekers,

This is a week to rise early and seek the shade. The programming calendar is quiet, so the practice is the weather: a heat wave builds Sunday through Thursday to a 95° peak Wednesday, which makes the cool dawn hours and the park's shaded, contemplative corners the whole point. The free Gottesman Pool is your daily reset.

## Weather this week

Storms Friday and showers Saturday in the low-80s, then a hot, still, sunny stretch Sunday through Thursday to a 95° peak Wednesday. Claim the first hour after sunrise every day — light on the Mall, mist on the Reservoir, cool air in the Ramble — because by late morning the heat makes stillness hard to find anywhere but deep shade.

## Fri Jul 10 — a rinsed, quiet park between storms

The rain keeps the crowds thin and the air soft. Between cells, the Conservatory Garden (north end) and Hallett Nature Sanctuary are at their most private. **Gottesman Pool** offers free 7 AM lap swim up at the Harlem Meer — a cool, meditative way to start.

## Sat Jul 11 — solitude on the north and west sides

A showery, low-80s morning is ideal for a slow, near-empty walk through the North Woods, the Ravine, or Hallett. One heads-up so you can avoid the crowd: a race fills the lower loop 7:30–10 AM and SummerStage crowds gather south of 72nd in the afternoon — so the **north end is your quiet half** all day.

## Sun Jul 12 — go early, before the festivals

The south and mid-park get busy midday (a Bandshell festival, a SummerStage concert), so take your dawn practice at the Reservoir or in the Conservatory Garden and be finished before 11. First hot day at 85° — the early window matters.

## Mon Jul 13 — 86°, dawn practice at the Reservoir

No programming; just you and the light. The Reservoir track at sunrise and the Conservatory Garden right at opening are the week's calmest spaces. Retreat before the heat builds.

## Tue Jul 14 — 91°, shade and water

Hot. Move your practice into the deepest shade — the Ramble, the Ravine, Summit Rock — or into the water at Gottesman Pool's 7 AM lap swim. Keep it slow and hydrated.

## Wed Jul 15 — 95°, the peak; stillness at first light only

The hottest day of the week. A sunrise sit at Turtle Pond or a slow Ramble wander, then indoors. This is not a day to push — gentle, brief, and early is the whole practice.

## Thu Jul 16 — 92°, a calm close

Still hot but easing. End the week with a dawn walk somewhere quiet — Hallett or the North Woods — and let the heat wave's edge soften. The park is yours before 8.

## Quick recap

- **Gottesman Pool free lap swim, 7 AM daily** — your cooling reset all week.
- **Heat wave Sun–Thu, 95° Wednesday** — practice in the first hour after sunrise, every day.
- **Quietest ground:** Conservatory Garden, Hallett, the Ramble, the Ravine, Summit Rock, the Reservoir at dawn.
- **Avoid midday south-of-72nd** Sat–Sun (race and festival crowds) — keep to the north end.

Breathe easy,
The Central Park Guide
