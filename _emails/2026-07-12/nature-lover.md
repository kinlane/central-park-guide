Hi nature lovers — a quiet week on the programming front, which makes it a week to follow the park's own rhythms: dawn in the Ramble, the woodland shade of the North Woods and the Loch, and the water's edge before the heat takes over. The story this week is the weather, and the wildlife will be reading it the same way you are.

## Weather this week
A heat wave peaks at 98°F on Wed 7/15 before easing into the low 90s and breaking with Saturday's showers. Dawn is your window all week — the dawn chorus, cooler air, and active birds are all before 8 AM; by midday, seek the deep shade of the Ramble, the Loch, and the Ravine, where the canopy and moving water keep things bearable.

## Mon–Wed — Dawn in the Ramble, heat by day
No scheduled nature programming, so let the park lead. Early mornings in the Ramble and around Turtle Pond are alive before the heat; the Loch's running water and the North Woods canopy are the coolest corners once the sun is up. On Wednesday's 98°F peak, keep it to a dawn outing and let the afternoon go.

## Thu Jul 16 — Turtle Pond & Belvedere at first light
Another dawn-favoring day at 91°F. Turtle Pond is at its best early — turtles basking, herons working the edges, dragonflies over the water — with Belvedere Castle above it. Skip the exposed Great Lawn later; a free concert fills it in the evening.

## Fri–Sat — Cooler air, the water's edge
As the heat eases Friday and cooler air arrives Saturday ahead of showers, the Reservoir and the Pool (at the park's north end) are worth a slow loop for waterbirds and the light on the water. Saturday morning is the pick of the week before storms possibly arrive mid-afternoon.

## Sat Jul 18 — Model boats & a Great Hill campout
Two gentle, nature-adjacent notes: model-yacht racing on Conservatory Water at Kerbs Boathouse (9 AM), and the Urban Park Rangers' Family Camping overnight on the Great Hill (7 PM) — a rare chance to experience the park's north woods after dark. Both are lovely if you like your nature with a little human warmth.

## Sun Jul 19 — Damp and green
Cooler and unsettled with lingering shower chances — which is exactly when the Ramble and North Woods smell best and the birds move low. A fine morning for a slow, wet-weather ramble if you don't mind damp boots.

## Quick recap
- **Dawn is the week** — chorus and active wildlife before 8 AM; deep shade (Ramble, Loch, Ravine) by day.
- **Wed 7/15 peaks at 98°F** — a dawn outing only.
- **Water's edge:** Reservoir and the Pool best Fri–Sat as the air cools.
- **Gentle extras Sat:** model boats at Kerbs (9 AM), Rangers' overnight on the Great Hill (7 PM).

Keep looking up,
The Central Park Guide
