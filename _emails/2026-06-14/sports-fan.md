# Central Park Sports — Mon Jun 15 – Sun Jun 21

Hey sports fans,

This is a diamond week. Little League and youth baseball fill North Meadow, Heckscher Fields, and the Great Lawn most days, adult baseball takes over North Meadow on Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday, and there's kickball at Heckscher every single day. Soccer training runs the weekday mornings, the Central Park Sports Summer Program keeps the kids busy, and tennis lights up Lincoln Terrace at the weekend. If you only watch one thing, make it the water: Model Yacht Racing returns to Conservatory Water on Saturday 6/20 at 9 AM — the week's signature event and one of the best small spectacles the park serves up.

One quick note for the running and cycling crowd before we get to the fields: the loop is clean all week. No NYRR races, no crit, nothing staging on East, West, or Center Drive any day. Rare and worth knowing.

## Weather this week

Monday through Wednesday sits in the upper-70s — ideal playing weather for the fields and the courts — before Thursday spikes to 87°F with thunderstorms likely, which puts rain delays and cancellations on the table for the adult ball and the kickball slate. The weekend turns hot and sunny in the mid-80s, so the day games and the Model Yacht Racing run warm — hydrate, bring shade, and plan around the afternoon sun.

## The diamonds

The center of the week. Youth and Little League baseball run most days across North Meadow, Heckscher Fields, and the Great Lawn — expect full slates Monday through Wednesday while the weather is prime. Adult baseball claims North Meadow on Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday; Thursday's the one to watch the radar on, with storms in the forecast that could push or wipe the games. Kickball is a daily fixture at Heckscher Fields all week long. Field assignments shuffle day to day, but North Meadow and the Great Lawn diamonds are your reliable hubs — show up and you'll find a game.

## Soccer and the summer program

James Christie Soccer Training runs the weekday mornings, and the Central Park Sports Summer Program is on weekdays too, so the youth side of things keeps the fields humming Monday through Friday even around the baseball. Good week to catch the next generation putting in the work.

## Tennis

The courts come alive at the weekend over at Lincoln Terrace. Youth tennis runs Friday, and open tennis takes Saturday — the warm, sunny weekend weather is on your side for it, just plan for heat on the hard courts come midday.

## Water

The week's headliner: Model Yacht Racing, Saturday 6/20 at 9 AM, Conservatory Water by the Kerbs Boathouse. Hand-built boats, real competition, and a morning slot that beats the worst of the weekend heat. If you've never watched it, this is your week — grab a coffee and a bench on the east shore.

## Field closures to plan around

A few fields go offline for maintenance, so route around them:

- Bowling Green is closed Tuesday and Friday for maintenance.
- East Meadow is closed Saturday and Sunday.
- Everything else — North Meadow and Great Lawn diamonds especially — stays active.

## Quick recap

**It's a diamond week.** Youth and Little League baseball most days at North Meadow, Heckscher, and the Great Lawn; adult baseball Thu/Sat/Sun at North Meadow; kickball daily at Heckscher.

**Model Yacht Racing is the must-watch.** Saturday 6/20, 9 AM, Conservatory Water by Kerbs Boathouse — the week's signature event.

**Thursday is the rain-delay risk.** 87°F and thunderstorms likely could pause or cancel games; the rest of the week plays clean.

**Tennis takes the weekend.** Youth Friday, open Saturday, both at Lincoln Terrace.

**Field closures:** Bowling Green out Tue and Fri, East Meadow out Sat–Sun — North Meadow and the Great Lawn stay open.

**Note for runners and cyclists:** the loop is clean all week — no races, no crit, no closures.
