# Central Park Running Heads-Up: Week of May 3–9

Hey runners,

A few things on the Central Park calendar this week are going to get in your way. Here's what to know so you can plan your routes.

## Today (Sun May 3) — the big one

The TD Five Boro Bike Tour finish takes over **Center Drive and East Drive from 7 AM to noon**. Most of the lower and east loop is a no-go until early afternoon. Your options:

- Run the **Bridle Path** or the **Reservoir loop** instead
- Push your run to **after 1 PM**, when things should be reopening
- Heads up: Bandshell Plaza has the Arise New York event noon–6 PM, so expect West Drive crowds in the afternoon too

## Mon–Fri (May 4–8) — mornings at Engineers' Gate

The Frederick Law Olmsted Luncheon is set up at the **5th Ave sidewalk at 97th / Duke Ellington Circle from midnight to noon every day this week**. If you usually enter the Reservoir at Engineers' Gate (90th & 5th), you'll hit congestion. **Enter at 86th or 96th instead** and you won't notice it.

## Wed May 6 afternoon — Reservoir north side

There's an event setup at **North Reservoir Landscape A from 2–5 PM**, right next to the track. Probably fine, but expect some foot traffic on the north end during that window.

## Sat May 9 afternoon — West Drive at 72nd

**Central Park Skate Circle** rolls in at Dead Road North from **2–7 PM**. The crowd spills toward West Drive near 72nd. Run earlier in the day or stick to the upper park.

## Quick recap

**Sun 5/3, 7 AM–noon — Center & East Drive**
Run the Bridle Path or Reservoir, or wait until 1 PM.

**Mon–Fri mornings — Engineers' Gate (90th & 5th)**
Enter the Reservoir at 86th or 96th instead.

**Wed 5/6, 2–5 PM — Reservoir north side**
Run earlier in the day, or pick a different loop.

**Sat 5/9, 2–7 PM — West Drive at 72nd**
Run in the morning, or stay in the upper park.

The calmest windows this week look like **weekday early mornings (after the Bike Tour)** and **Sat morning before noon**. Have a good week out there.

See you on the loop,
The Central Park Guide
