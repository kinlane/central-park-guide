# Central Park Sports This Week: May 10–16

Hey sports fans,

This is a varied sports week — three races, lots of baseball, model yachts, and the Skate Circle returning. Here's what's worth catching.

## Today (Sun May 10) — busy day on every field

**Baseball is everywhere again.** Today's lineup:

- **North Meadow Baseball fields 1, 3, 4, 5, 6, 10, 11** — adult and Little League games running 8 AM to 8 PM (yes, multiple fields til 8 PM)
- **Great Lawn Softball fields 1–8** — 12U Little League most of the day
- **North Meadow Softball-12** — adult softball, 10 AM–2 PM
- **Heckscher Fields Softball-03** — adult kickball league, 10 AM–7:30 PM
- **Heckscher Fields Softball-01, -04** — Little League afternoon games

**Race of Hope NYC** runs on the 102nd Street Cross Drive, 8:30 AM–noon. Small-ish 5K, worth a watch if you're at the north end.

**Skate Circle** returns at Dead Road North, 2–7 PM. Less sport, more spectacle, but always worth a look when it activates.

## Tue May 12 — Bowling Green closed for maintenance

If you go to watch University Club lawn bowling, **Bowling Green is closed all day Tuesday** for maintenance. Also closed until 2 PM Friday. Otherwise the green's open this week.

## Wed May 13 — Pedals of Honor 2026

A charity cycling event on the **102nd Street Cross Drive, 9 AM–3 PM** — honors fallen military and first responders. Cyclists at every level, often emotional finish. Worth watching if you're in the area.

## Sat May 16 — early racing + model yachts

- **CRCA Central Park Racing Series**, East Drive, **4 AM–8:30 AM.** Saturday-morning bike crit racing. Get coffee at 6 AM and post up on East Drive — the peloton flies past, riders working hard, real racing energy.
- **Model Yacht Racing** at Kerbs Boathouse, 9 AM–3 PM. Remote-controlled sailboats on Conservatory Water. The Central Park Model Yacht Club's regular Saturday race day. Goofy and lovely.
