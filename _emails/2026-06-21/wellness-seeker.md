# Central Park Wellness — Mon Jun 22 – Sun Jun 28

*Your Sunday week-ahead look, covering Mon Jun 22 through Sun Jun 28.*

Hi wellness seekers — no scheduled classes on the calendar this week, so this one's about finding your own quiet and your own light. The early-week rain hands you hushed, empty contemplative spaces; the dry midweek mornings are made for a slow practice; and the weekend asks you to go early and stay north to dodge the heat and the crowds.

## Weather this week

Storms Monday (76°) and showers Tuesday (79°) clear to calm, sunny days Wednesday (83°) and Thursday (84°), then it turns hot and humid for the weekend (82° Friday, 86° Saturday with afternoon storm chances). Your best practice windows are the Wed–Thu dawns; on the hot weekend days, claim sunrise and the shaded gardens before the day heats up.

## Mon 6/22 — Rain, and the gift of an empty park

Showers and thunderstorms, 76°. A day to embrace the weather rather than fight it — the **Conservatory Garden and the Ramble are nearly empty in the rain**, and a slow walk under the North Woods canopy is its own meditation. If you practice indoors, save it for today.

## Tue 6/23 — Clearing skies, quiet water

Rain showers easing, 79°. As the wet lifts, the **Hallett Nature Sanctuary and the Pool's north shore** are still and restorative. Turtle Pond below Belvedere Castle is a calm midday sit if the showers have passed.

## Wed 6/24 — Sunrise practice at its best

83° and sunny — the week's finest morning. Catch first light on the **Reservoir or from Summit Rock**, then settle into the **Conservatory Garden** for a quiet sit among the peaking perennials. (Note: Bow Bridge has a film shoot from 1 PM, so for stillness, stay with the gardens and the north end that afternoon.)

## Thu 6/25 — Calm and clear

Mostly sunny, 84°. Another excellent dawn — the Ramble, Hallett, and the North Woods are at their most serene before the city wakes. A slow Reservoir loop at sunrise is a fine reset.

## Fri 6/26 – Sat 6/27 — Claim the early hours

Warm and unsettled Friday (82°), hot Saturday (86°). On both days the move is the same: **be out at sunrise and into the shade** — the Conservatory Garden's allées, the Ramble's interior, Summit Rock — before the heat and weekend crowds arrive. One location heads-up: a morning footrace runs on the park drives Saturday (~7:30–10 AM), so the Reservoir and loop edge are busier early — the gardens and the north end are your calm. If you like a gentle guided walk, the Conservancy's **"Iconic Views" tour runs Fri at 10 AM.**

## Sun 6/28 — Go north, go early

Summer warmth, possible afternoon storm. A morning race runs on the drives (~7:30–10:30 AM) and the south end gets loud this afternoon with a big concert at Rumsey, so make this a **morning-and-north day**: sunrise at the Reservoir's quieter north gate, then the Conservatory Garden, the Pool, and the North Woods for solitude while the crowds gather downtown.

## Quick recap

- **No scheduled classes this week** — self-guided quiet and light.
- **Wed 6/24 + Thu 6/25 dawns** are the best practice windows.
- **Rainy Mon–Tue** give you an empty Conservatory Garden and Ramble.
- **Hot Sat–Sun:** sunrise and shade — gardens, Ramble interior, Summit Rock.
- **Sun 6/28:** head north for solitude; a concert fills the Rumsey area in the afternoon.

Breathe easy,
— Central Park Guide
