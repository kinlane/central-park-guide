# Central Park Wellness Week: Fri May 15 – Fri May 22

Hi there,

New Friday-evening edition. No scheduled yoga or pilates classes on the park calendar this week, but the **Mon–Thu stretch is one of the calmest in months** — ideal for self-directed practice or a long, slow walk. Here's the week.

## Where to find calm

These spots are undisturbed all week (or nearly so):

- **Conservatory Garden** (105th & 5th) — peonies and irises now; visually rich and quiet
- **Hallett Nature Sanctuary** — small, contemplative; check posted hours
- **The Ramble** — early-morning bird song
- **North Woods & Ravine** — waterfalls still flowing strong
- **Mineral Springs** — busy Sat–Sun for Sacred Sites; quiet Mon–Thu and beautiful around dusk

## Sat May 16 — Sacred Sites Open House Day 1

**Sacred Sites Open House at Mineral Springs, 10 AM–3 PM.** Citywide program opening historic park structures for guided tours. Slow, attentive, story-rich — the contemplative cousin of architecture-as-wellness. Day 2 runs Sun May 17, same time.

If you're looking for something that feels like wellness without being formal yoga or pilates, this is the pick of the week.

## Things to avoid for peace

- **Sat 5/16 morning, East Drive** — CRCA bike racing 4–8:30 AM
- **Sat 5/16 daytime** — south/southwest of park is busy with the Food Market + multiple events
- **Sat 5/16 afternoon, near Levin Playground** — PS 77 Spring Festival 2–5 PM
- **Bow Bridge, Bethesda, Cop Cot, Ladies' Pavilion** Saturday and Sunday — full of private ceremonies

## Best self-practice windows this week

- **Sat 5/16 sunrise** — quiet from 5–7 AM before the racing energy fully arrives
- **Sun 5/17 sunrise** — calmest of the weekend mornings
- **Mon–Thu mornings (5/18–5/21)** — full week of quiet. Pick any sunrise.
- **Any evening** — sunset around 8 PM, twilight light is at its best now

## A gentle suggestion

This is the kind of week where nothing on the calendar matches your wellness need — and that's the gift. **Make your own.** Bring a mat to Conservatory Garden, find a bench. Sit for 20 minutes. Walk the Reservoir. Drink coffee at Mineral Springs around sunset on a weekday.

The park can be a structured wellness venue, but more often it's the space that hosts the practice you bring to it. This week is for that.
