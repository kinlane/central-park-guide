# Central Park Walking Plans — Sat Jun 6 – Fri Jun 12

Hi walkers — a full week of evening events to walk toward: Naumburg Tuesday, NY Phil Wednesday, Yellowcard Thursday, and the 40-in-Focus photography exhibit opening Friday. Monday at 79°F is the week's best all-day strolling weather.

## Weather this week

93°F Saturday, 87°F Sunday, 79°F Monday, 85°F Tuesday, 90°F Wednesday, 92°F Thursday and Friday. Morning walks before 10 AM are comfortable all week; afternoon walks require shade and water after Monday.

## Sat 6/6 — Mini 10K morning energy, then a full afternoon (93°F)

**Mastercard Mini 10K** on East Drive 8–10:30 AM — excellent spectating near 72nd Street or Engineers' Gate, but avoid East Drive as a walking route until after 11 AM. After 93°F arrives, mornings-only is the rule.

Saturday's worth walking toward:
- **Model Yacht Racing** at Kerbs Boathouse (Conservatory Water), 9 AM–3 PM.
- **Sing for Hope Choir Fest** at The Dairy, 12–3 PM.
- **Tango at Shakespeare Statue**, 6–9 PM — worth the evening return trip.

East Meadow and Bowling Green Lawn both closed all day.

## Sun 6/7 — World Ocean Day, Shakespeare at two venues (87°F)

- **World Ocean Day Art Relay** at Heckscher Playground Lawns, 9 AM–3 PM.
- **Sicily. Lands in Hands** at Cherry Hill, 10 AM–1 PM — a stop on any south-end walk.
- **Julius Caesar** at The Pool Lawns, 5–10 PM.
- **Barefoot Shakespeare** at Summit Rock, 3–8 PM.
- International Folk Dancers meet Sunday afternoon at the east-side band circle.

87°F — walk the shaded paths through the Ramble or along the Bridle Path if you're out midday.

## Mon 6/8 — Best walking day of the week (79°F)

Clear, 79°F, no major events until the DJ Ali Bandshell Rave at 3–7 PM. Walk anywhere, any time before noon. Bow Bridge, Bethesda Terrace, the Reservoir, the Ramble — it's all open and uncrowded on a Monday morning. The Bandshell rave in the afternoon adds energy to the central corridor if you want to wander past.

## Tue 6/9 — Naumburg concert evening (85°F)

**Baroklyn, Simone Dinnerstein & CONCORA** at the Naumburg Bandshell, 7:30 PM (free). Walk from the south end along the Mall to the Bandshell, arrive by 7 PM, stay for the concert. One of the week's cleanest walk-and-event pairings. Julius Caesar continues at The Pool Lawns (5–10 PM) for those heading to the upper west side.

## Wed 6/10 — NY Philharmonic at the Great Lawn (8 PM, 90°F)

The event of the week. Arrive early (5–6 PM) to claim a lawn spot. 90°F midday makes a late-afternoon arrival more practical — come in from 5 PM and walk north through the park to the Great Lawn. The paths between 72nd Street and the Great Lawn will be shoulder-to-shoulder by 7 PM; that's the point.

## Thu 6/11 — Yellowcard SummerStage, 92°F

**Yellowcard / New Found Glory / Plain White T's** at Rumsey Playfield, 6–10 PM (ticketed). Even if you're not attending, the Rumsey area is lively from the afternoon. Morning walks only in 92°F heat; evening is concert crowd plus residual heat.

## Fri 6/12 — 40-in-Focus opens at the Arsenal; Carlos Konig Salsa at Bandshell 5–8 PM

**40 in Focus: SummerStage through the Lens of Jack Vartoogian** opens at the Arsenal (64th Street & 5th Avenue), 9 AM–5 PM — free. A 40-year photographic record of SummerStage, worth an hour of your day. Then the **Carlos Konig Salsa** at Bandshell Plaza (5–8 PM) bookends the afternoon. Julius Caesar continues at The Pool Lawns (5–10 PM). NY State Golf Croquet Championship runs all day at Bowling Green Lawn — an unexpectedly charming spectacle if you wander past.

## Quick recap

- **Sat 6/6:** Mini 10K east side 8–10:30 AM — spectate or walk west; 93°F, go early.
- **Mon 6/8:** Best walking day — 79°F, every path open.
- **Tue 6/9:** Naumburg 7:30 PM — walk + concert.
- **Wed 6/10:** NY Phil 8 PM — arrive 5–6 PM; 90°F midday, so come later.
- **Fri 6/12:** 40-in-Focus opens at Arsenal 9 AM; Carlos Konig Salsa at Bandshell 5–8 PM.

Enjoy the week,
— Central Park Guide
