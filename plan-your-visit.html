Plan your park day your way

No two visits are the same. Pick the path that fits you.

For runners

  • 3-Mile Loop: Lower loop from Columbus Circle to Bethesda Terrace to back.
  • 6-Mile Loop: Full park drive loop, the classic. Start anywhere, end where you like.
  • Long Run (10+ miles): Add Harlem Hill repeats or extend into the East/West Drives for distance.

For families

  • Carousel: A vintage favorite, open spring through fall near 65th Street.
  • Playgrounds: Heckscher Playground (biggest in the park) and Ancient Playground (next to the Met).
  • Picnic spots: Sheep Meadow (open space + skyline views), Great Hill (quiet lawns, uptown).
  • Bonus: Rowboats at Loeb Boathouse are a family crowd-pleaser.

For first-time visitors

  • Bow Bridge: The most photographed spot in the park -- classic views.
  • Strawberry Fields: Tribute to John Lennon, with live musicians most days.
  • The Mall & Literary Walk: Historic elm trees and statues of writers.
  • Bethesda Terrace & Fountain: Iconic arches, tiled ceiling, and water views.
  • Hidden gem: Conservatory Garden (formal gardens, seasonal blooms).

For dog lovers

  • Off-Leash Hours: 6 AM-9 AM & 9 PM-1 AM on all lawns/wooded areas.
  • Dog runs: Bull Moose Dog Run (west side, 81st St.) and Peter Detmold Dog Run (east side, 49th St.).
  • Best strolls: The Ramble (wooded trails for adventurous pups) and Harlem Meer (calm waterside paths).
  • Tip: Bring water! Fountains with dog basins are scattered but seasonal.

You might even spot Poppy

Every great park has characters. Ours is four-legged, rides sidecar, and answers to Poppy. Keep an eye out, the Squirrel Patrol might roll past your picnic.
